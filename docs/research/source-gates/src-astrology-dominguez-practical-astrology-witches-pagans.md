## Gate questions

### 1. Is this actually a ritual-use astrology source, or mostly interpretation/correspondence material?

It is a ritual-use astrology source with substantial interpretive foundation.

The opening frames astrology as a sacred symbolic language and a technology that can be applied to magick, not merely divination or personality exploration. The book teaches core concepts because later chapters apply them to spellwork, rituals, timing, glyphs, magick squares, and adaptation to astrological conditions.

It is not merely interpretation/correspondence material, though it contains interpretation and correspondence content that should be used carefully.

### 2. Does it provide mechanics for turning computed astrology into ritual action?

Yes.

Mechanics include:

- learning planetary/sign/aspect glyphs as magical alphabet;
- using glyphs in magical work;
- using astrological timing and the cycles of time;
- choosing a time for magickal or spiritual work;
- considering Moon phase, Moon sign, void-of-course Moon, aspects, planetary days, planetary hours, and transits;
- adapting to imperfect timing by emphasizing supportive cycles, planes of action, sacred-space/casting work, and lesser influences;
- using physical representations, verbal calls, and symbolic marks to strengthen planetary hours;
- deriving focal symbols from magick squares of the seven elder planets.

### 3. Does it support planetary contacts, planetary days/hours, natal/transit timing, or electional timing?

Yes, with limits.

Strong support:

- planetary days/hours;
- Moon phase and Moon sign timing;
- void-of-course Moon cautions;
- aspects as timing windows before culmination;
- planetary order sequence;
- adapting to current conditions;
- use of natal charts and transits as patterns, but not as deterministic personality output.

Electional timing support is practical and introductory, not a full specialist electional system. Treat it as household ritual timing support, not a full electional app.

### 4. Does it provide planet-by-planet ritual logic for Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn?

Yes.

Chapter 4 covers the planets and provides planet-by-planet symbolic logic, including the seven elder planets central to magical practice. Appendix A provides planet and sign correspondences, and Appendix C provides magick squares for the seven elder planets. This makes the source useful for planet-specific ritual families, with extraction limits.

Yes, if extraction is disciplined.

The source is useful because it treats astrology as a symbolic and timing language for magickal action. It can help Moon & Table say why a waxing Moon, a Venus hour, a Mercury aspect, or a Saturn condition changes the shape of a ritual.

The extraction risk is horoscope drift: the app must not turn the source into natal personality readings, deterministic forecasts, or transit advice. Use the source for timing-to-practice translation.
