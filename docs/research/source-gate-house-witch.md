# Source gate record: The House Witch

## Purpose of this revision

This revision corrects the source-gate posture for Moon & Table.

The prior packet was too conservative around words, blessings, prayers, invocations, prompts, recipes, spells, meditations, charms, and ritual language. Those are not peripheral. They are often the ritual.

Moon & Table is a private household ritual calendar / grimoire for Tim and Jessica. The purpose is not to publish derivative ritual content to the public. The source gate should therefore distinguish between:

1. **mechanics extraction** — source-backed ritual structure, materials, sequence, timing, carrier, purpose, and safety context;
2. **private exact-text use** — exact wording manually selected by Tim from a source he has provided/possesses and stored privately in Moon & Table;
3. **assistant-generated output** — generated packets should not reproduce long copyrighted passages wholesale.

The corrected posture is:

> Words, prayers, blessings, invocations, prompts, meditations, spells, charms, and recipes are valid Ritual mechanics and may be central to future Moon & Table Rituals. Exact source wording may be permitted for private household use when manually selected by Tim from his copy, cited, and kept private. Assistant-generated research packets should identify where exact wording matters and how it may be stored, but should not reproduce long copyrighted passages.

## Practical meaning

Allowed:

* Tim may use exact words from his own copy in his private app.
* Short operative source wording of 20 words or fewer may be used verbatim when reviewed, product-safe, cited, and non-substitutive.
* Longer operative source wording must be adapted into Moon & Table words in the ritual body and tracked as adapted source wording.
* Future extraction may flag exact wording as requiring 20-word/adapted-word review.

Not allowed by default:

* Research agents should not generate long copied passages from modern copyrighted sources.
* Exact source wording should not be committed to a public repository.
* Exact source wording should not become public app copy.
* Exact source wording should not be broadly reusable runtime content.
* Exact source wording should not be marked recommendable/direct-use eligible until human review.
