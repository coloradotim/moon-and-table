## Gap notes

- `plant + tending` is the strongest lane. The source repeatedly treats everyday care as spiritual practice.
- `plant + remembering` is also very strong through garden journals, plant records, growth notes, pressed records, photos, seasonal comparison, and plant pages.
- `plant + opening` is strong through existing plants, new plants, seeds/cuttings, kitchen-scrap regrowth, windowsill/container practice, first shoots, and plant welcome.
- `plant + marking` is stronger after this revision because plant stage, first shoot, photos, repotting, seasonal blessing, harvest, and growth records are all accounted for.
- `plant + releasing` is stronger after this revision because decomposition, compost, dead leaf, pruning, rededication, and clearing plant space are all tracked.
- `plant + voicing` is real but should be gentle: greeting, thanking, speaking a watering word, asking before taking, asking what is shown. Do not overstate plant communication.
- `plant + connecting` should be treated as supported but carefully worded: living relationship, household witness, offering attention, and non-depleting energy sharing.
- `plant + protecting` is usable but moderate; avoid guaranteed magical warding and named risky/protective plants until screened.
- `vessel` is a major secondary carrier: pot, planter, terrarium, jar, seed tray, container, plant record sleeve, and record binder.
- `table` is a meaningful secondary carrier for plant corner, plant table, journal table, altar/workspace, and domestic setup.
- `doorway` is secondary and should be used mainly for window/threshold/entry plant placement, not public-land gardening.
- `candlelight` remains unsupported for normal extraction from this source. Held references remain held.
