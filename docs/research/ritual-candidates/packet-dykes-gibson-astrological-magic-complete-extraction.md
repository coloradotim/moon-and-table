# Astrological Magic Complete Extraction Packet

Source: Benjamin N. Dykes and Jayne B. Gibson, _Astrological Magic: Basic Rituals and Meditations_  
Packet status: `ready_for_human_review_before_import`  
Extraction stance: complete mapping pass for the named rites. This source is highly ceremonial, timing-aware, and correspondence-heavy; complete rites are approved as findable/direct-use candidates, while elections, tables, figures, colors, incenses, and alternative divine-name lists are preserved as metadata/support.

## Import Doctrine Applied

Moon & Table imports self-contained source-backed Rituals. The named elemental, planetary, and zodiacal rites have a beginning/container, middle/core invocation and consecration work, and closing. They are high-capacity and often require prerequisite objects or timing, but that affects recommendation eligibility, not whether they can exist as findable Ritual records.

The source includes long spoken invocations and formal ceremonial language. Import should transform the user-facing ritual bodies into Moon & Table house voice and avoid copying long source text. Source lineage should remain traceable through title, source location, and metadata.

- Source location: Part 1, Earth rite, pp. 57-64
- Import readiness: `approved_for_mechanical_import`
- Direct-use target: `yes`
- Recommendation target: `yes`
- Primary purpose: consecrate salt as an elemental earth vessel
- Primary carrier: salt, altar, earth pentagram, tarot or visualization
- Audience: self
- Capacity: high
- Review labels: earth, salt, consecration, ceremonial, high_capacity, prerequisite_for_feast
- Recommendation constraints: recommend only when user has room for something deeper and asks for grounding, embodiment, stability, or elemental earth work
- Timing notes: helpful when earth sign, earth triplicity, or strong earth timing is present; not required
- Headline: Consecrate Salt for Earth

- Intention: I give Earth a clean vessel and let form steady me.
- Question: What in me wants weight, patience, and a place to stand?
