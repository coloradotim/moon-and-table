# The Master Book of Candle Burning Complete Extraction Packet

Source: Henri Gamache, _The Master Book of Candle Burning_  
Packet status: `ready_for_human_review_before_import`  
Extraction stance: complete mapping pass, over-inclusive for ritual-shaped candle work. Enemy, coercive, confusion, separation, and medically-adjacent workings are preserved as findable/direct-use candidates with recommendation constraints rather than excluded.

## Import Doctrine Applied

Moon & Table imports self-contained source-backed Rituals. This source is a candle-burning manual built around a fixed altar layout, Psalm readings, astral/offertory/symbolic candles, and timed candle movement. The named exercises are ritual-shaped even when their magical aim is adversarial or coercive. Color lists, candle catalogs, scripture lists, diagrams, and brand/oil references are mapped as support unless they form a performable beginning, middle, and close.

### `gamache-candle-burning-altar`

- Source location: altar diagram and setup, pp. 33-36
- Import readiness: `approved_for_mechanical_import`
- Direct-use target: `yes`
- Recommendation target: `yes`
- Primary purpose: create a candle-burning altar container
- Primary carrier: altar table, altar candles, Bible or sacred book, dressed candle
- Audience: self
- Capacity: medium
- Review labels: candle, altar, preparation, psalm_optional
- Headline: Set the Candle-Burning Table
- Summary: A source-backed altar container for candle work, using back altar lights, a central working space, and a dressed candle.
- Practice: Clear a heat-safe table. Place two altar lights at the back and leave room for the working candles in front. Add a Bible or sacred book if that belongs to the work. Dress the working candle with a fitting oil or a plain substitute, moving the oil in the direction of the spell. Light the altar candles first, name the work, then light the working candle.
- Intention: I give the candle work a table, a direction, and a beginning.
- Question: What changed once the work had a real place to stand?

- Summary: A short candle preparation ritual using oil, touch, and direction before a candle is burned.

## Completeness Challenge

I challenged this extraction against likely under-extraction patterns:

- Named exercises: all 22 exercises were mapped rather than filtering out coercive, adversarial, medical-adjacent, or relationship-interference work.
- Technique chapters: altar setup and candle dressing were extracted because they are performable ritual architectures.
- Tables/catalogs: held as metadata/support rather than inflated into weak standalone Rituals.
- Old source wording: headlines, summaries, practices, intentions, and questions were transformed into Moon & Table house voice.
- Recommendation separation: ritual-shaped records remain findable/direct-use even when they are not appropriate for `Choose with me` yet.

## House Voice / Metadata QA

- Headlines name the action in Moon & Table language rather than copying exercise titles verbatim.
- Practice bodies preserve the ritual logic: altar, represented candles, direction/movement, sacred text if used, repeated burn, close/record.
- Coercive and adversarial records are not sanitized into generic protection; they are honestly labeled and recommendation-held.
- Medical-adjacent language avoids treatment claims and keeps ordinary care beside the ritual.
- Psalm/Bible elements are represented as optional sacred-text lineage without copying long passages.
