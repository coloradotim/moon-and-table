## Source notes

### SN-WHITEHURST-FLOWER-SELECTION

Use flower selection as a ritual act, not a database lookup. Whitehurst supports correspondences, but the approved gate says affinity, availability, local flowers, and direct flower relationship matter. Runtime should not say: "you need this exact flower." It should say: "choose this flower if it is safe and present, or use a reviewed substitute / image / simple white flower."

### SN-WHITEHURST-CORRESPONDENCE-SUPPORT

Appendices B-D may support source-note cards after review. They should not be copied wholesale, exposed as a browseable database, or used to generate long lists. Use them to explain why a chosen flower fits a purpose/timing when the actual ritual candidate is already reviewed.

### SN-WHITEHURST-SAFETY-FIRST-FLOWER

Before direct-use review, every runtime flower must have a household safety check: pets, children, pregnancy, allergies, fragrance sensitivity, irritating sap, toxicity, access, sprayed/treated florist materials, and disposal. Until then, candidates may be reviewed conceptually but remain unavailable.

### SN-WHITEHURST-FLOWER-AS-WITNESS

The strongest Moon & Table shape is not "flower X means Y." It is: a flower is placed where the household can see it; it receives a short spoken or silent purpose; it witnesses a moment; it is thanked; it is returned or preserved.
