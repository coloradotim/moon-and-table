## Source notes

### SN-WHITEHURST-FLOWER-SELECTION

Use flower selection as a ritual act, not a database lookup. Affinity, availability, local flowers, and direct flower relationship matter. Use correspondences as support only.

### SN-WHITEHURST-CORRESPONDENCE-SUPPORT

Appendices B-D may support source-note cards after review. They should not be copied wholesale, exposed as a browseable database, or used to generate long lists.

### SN-WHITEHURST-SAFETY-FIRST-FLOWER

Before direct-use review, every runtime flower must have a household safety check: pets, children, household sensitivity, allergies, fragrance sensitivity, irritating sap, toxicity, access, sprayed/treated florist materials, and disposal. Until then, candidates may be reviewed conceptually but remain unavailable.

### SN-WHITEHURST-FLOWER-AS-WITNESS

The strongest Moon & Table shape is not `flower X means Y.` It is: a flower is placed where the household can see it; it receives a short spoken or silent purpose; it witnesses a moment; it is thanked; it is returned or preserved.

### SN-WHITEHURST-DO-NOT-PAD

The matrix is used to catch gaps, not to force every cell. Doorway/body/candlelight cells remain secondary, weak, or held unless the source gives a safe flower-first practice.
