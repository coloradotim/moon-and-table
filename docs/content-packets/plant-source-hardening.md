## 1. Research verdict

**PM verdict:** broad #233 should **not** proceed as one large Plant implementation issue yet. The Plant lane is emotionally important and usable, but only a few subfamilies are ready enough to implement safely. Split #233 or constrain the first pass to source-pinned, low-risk mechanics.

**Ready or nearly ready for narrow implementation:**

- **Rosemary / evergreen remembrance** — `implementation-ready with restrictions`. Rosemary is strongly pinned as remembrance through Shakespeare, Victorian floriography context, and modern commemoration practice. Use as symbolic remembrance / continuity only. Do not use medicinal, purification, luck, protection, fertility, or ingestive claims.
- **Dead-leaf / seasonal rest / dormancy boundaries** — `implementation-ready with restrictions`. This is best supported by horticultural care guidance rather than folklore: winter/low-light rest, reduced growth, leaf drop, and avoiding unnecessary intervention. Use for "let the plant rest / remove only what is clearly dead / do not force care" mechanics.
- **Window / sill plant as household place** — `implementation-ready after exact source pinning`. Window boxes and town/window gardening are documented, but the ritual use is mostly architectural/domestic placement rather than magical practice. This can support place-based Plant + Home recommendations after a tighter exact source pass.

**Promising but not ready:**

- **Plant witness** — promising, but not yet implementation-ready except as a very quiet "plant present on the table/window" mechanic. Strong exact sources for "plants as witnesses" were not found in this pass.
- **Plant as household companion** — promising but not ready. Public-domain domestic plant books can support plants-in-the-home and potted plant care, but "companion" language is interpretive unless better modern or folkloric sources are pinned.
- **Plant placement / return** — likely safe as mechanics, but source support is currently split between practical window gardening and symbolic plant lore. Mark `implementation-ready after exact source pinning`.

**Avoid/demote:**

- generic growth metaphor;
- watering/pruning as default ritual;
- ingestion;
- plant medicine;
- luck/protection/divination claims;
- toxic plant handling;
- culturally specific sacred plant practices without context;
- turning plant tending into chore/productivity reset.

## 6. Approved / restricted / avoid classifications

### Approved for narrow Part B implementation

1. **Rosemary remembrance**
   - Use: a small safe sprig, image, existing plant, or named rosemary presence to mark remembrance, continuity, or "what we want to keep in mind."
   - Boundaries: non-ingestive; no essential oils; no burning; no medicine; no protection/luck/purification/fertility claims.
   - Readiness: `implementation-ready with restrictions`.

2. **Seasonal plant rest / dormancy**
   - Use: leave a plant undisturbed, notice lowered growth, remove only a fully dead fallen leaf if appropriate, or return the plant to its usual place.
   - Boundaries: do not prescribe watering/pruning/repotting unless plant care is actually needed. Do not create chore language.
   - Readiness: `implementation-ready with restrictions`.

### Restricted / mechanics-only

1. **Window/sill plant**
   - Use: place an already-safe plant near a suitable window/sill briefly, or stand near a plant already in that location.
   - Boundaries: check light/cold/heat exposure; do not press leaves against cold glass; do not move fragile plants repeatedly.
   - Readiness: `implementation-ready after exact source pinning`.

2. **Plant placement / return**
   - Use: set a safe plant/object near a window/table for a short ritual moment, then return it to its usual place.
   - Boundaries: no "charging," no protection claims, no forced care.
   - Readiness: `implementation-ready after exact source pinning`.

3. **Plant witness**
   - Use later only if reframed as "with the plant present" rather than "the plant witnesses."
   - Readiness: `promising but not ready`.

### Avoid

- plant ingestion;
- plant tinctures, teas, waters, or "plant medicine";
- essential oils;
- smoke or burning herbs;
- luck/protection/divination claims;
- "growth mindset" metaphor with no plant material;
- touching unknown/toxic/irritating plants;
- using lilies, sago palm, oleander, foxglove, dieffenbachia, philodendron/pothos, euphorbia/spurge, or other known-risk plants without explicit safety gating;
- plant advice aimed at pregnancy, pets, children, or illness.

## 8. Safety restrictions

Plant safety must be treated as a content gate, not a user-facing lecture.

Moon & Table should:

- avoid specifying plant species unless the species has been safety-reviewed;
- avoid ingestion of any plant material;
- avoid herbal teas/tinctures/plant water unless a separate food/herb source packet approves it;
- avoid essential oils;
- avoid burning plant material;
- avoid plant handling for toxic, irritating, thorned, spined, or unknown plants;
- avoid recommendations involving lilies or other cat-dangerous plants;
- avoid moving plants to cold glass, heat vents, direct harsh sun, or unstable surfaces;
- avoid "water the plant" unless the plant actually needs water;
- avoid "prune the plant" unless the user has explicitly chosen plant care and the plant needs pruning;
- include internal safety flags for children, pets, pregnancy, allergies, and skin irritation.

The ASPCA Toxic and Non-Toxic Plants list is the primary pet-safety reference to consult before any species-specific Plant implementation. It states that the list covers plants reported to have systemic or intense gastrointestinal effects on animals, is not all-inclusive, and that any plant material can cause vomiting/GI upset in dogs and cats.

## 9. Cultural / religious / copyright cautions

- **Rosemary / ANZAC:** Treat ANZAC rosemary as evidence of rosemary's remembrance association, not as a ritual to copy.
- **Shakespeare:** Public domain, but do not copy the line into production ritual copy. Use as source support, not liturgy.
- **Victorian floriography:** Public-domain examples exist, but meanings vary. Treat as a symbolic vocabulary tradition, not objective truth.
- **Folkard:** Public domain, but may contain superstition, medicine, colonial-era framing, and culturally specific claims. Use only exact, bounded, safe excerpts.
- **Modern houseplant care articles:** Copyrighted and practical, not ritual. Use only for safety/material boundaries.
- **Indigenous/sacred plant practices:** Do not borrow without specific source context, permission/appropriateness review, and strong extraction boundaries.
