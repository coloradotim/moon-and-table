## Metaphysical Integrity Review

Safety should constrain recommendations; it should not rewrite the metaphysics of the practice.

Review questions:

- Does this preserve the ritual meaning of the practice?
- Did we accidentally reduce it to a wellness metaphor, visual prop, psychological cue, safety disclaimer, or decorative object?
- Are claims bounded without condescension?
- Are practical constraints handled quietly?
- Does the packet avoid telling the practitioner what to believe?

## Claims Boundaries

Do not make unsupported claims about:

- guaranteed protection
- guaranteed prosperity
- guaranteed love or relationship outcomes
- healing or medical effects
- legal outcomes
- physical safety outcomes
- certainty or prediction
- coercive influence
- attraction or manipulation

Allowed framing:

- symbolic support
- ritual attention
- household meaning
- source-backed material practice
- bounded invitation

## Codex Boundaries

Codex may:

- create draft packets from approved source candidates
- organize reviewed notes
- propose source-safe content
- implement active content only from `approved_for_implementation` packets

Codex must not:

- select sources independently as approved
- scrape or browse random sources as approved
- approve its own interpretations
- copy source text
- copy distinctive rituals, spells, prayers, chants, recipes, affirmations, petitions, or correspondence tables
- add private data
- weaken source, privacy, or practical constraints
- safety-wash magical practice
- tell practitioners what to believe
- add active content from a draft packet
- add new UI categories unless requested
