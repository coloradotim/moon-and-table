# High-capacity documented rite families research packet

Target path: `docs/content-packets/high-capacity-documented-rite-families.md`  
Issue: #227 — Research deeper documented rite families for steady/high-capacity recommendations  
Scope: source research and evaluation only. No SourceNotes, SymbolicCards, RitualPatterns, scoring, UI, or active ritual content should be implemented from this packet.

Core doctrine: **Source first. Pattern second.**

This revision hardens the first packet into a Part B implementation map. It keeps Candle/light first, downgrades Moon-specific and parts of Plant until better source pinning is completed, narrows pattern recommendations, and makes extraction boundaries explicit.

## 1. Research verdict

Moon & Table can safely deepen steady/high-capacity recommendations by adapting **documented rite families and ritual technologies**, not by inventing bigger rituals from vibes.

### Implementation-ready now

These lanes have enough source support to begin Part B implementation, provided extraction stays narrow and safety copy is built in:

- **#234 Candle/light: banked light, smooring, first light, renewed light, and safe witness light.** Strongest source base. The best primary anchor is Alexander Carmichael, *Carmina Gadelica*, Vol. 1, entries 82–85 and 87/88, especially entry 84, “Smooring the Fire,” which describes the practical hearth operation: spreading embers, forming a circle, covering them with ashes enough to subdue but not extinguish the fire, and keeping embers for morning use. Use mechanics only.
- **#231 Home/hearth/table: hearth settling and household object return.** Ready where it overlaps with the hearth/light sources. Keep it distinct from threshold fallback and cleaning/chore resets.
- **Limited Candle/light + Reflection witness forms.** A candle, lamp, or unlit candle can mark attention without divination, color systems, or flame interpretation.

### Implementation-ready with restrictions

- **Need-fire / new-fire mechanics.** Frazer documents extinguishing existing fires, making new fire, and carrying embers home to relight household hearths. However, the source is dated and embedded in disease, livestock, purification, and protection claims. Use only the abstract mechanics: old light out, one new safe light, return of light to the home.
- **Textual/folded/placed word mechanics.** Medieval textual amulet scholarship is strong as a ritual-technology lane, but exact accessible page/section pinning is not strong enough yet for active SourceNote implementation. Use mechanics only after source access is confirmed.
- **Kitchen shared vessel/table warmth.** Shared cup/bowl/table-center customs are promising and usable as mechanics, but avoid alcohol defaults, prosperity/harvest claims, food advice, and relationship-processing language.

### Promising but not ready

- **#236 Moon-specific lunar-material.** This lane is emotionally important and should remain separate from Candle/light, but the first packet overstated readiness. The exact source base for moonlit bowl/vessel/jar, moon-window contact, and moon-timed placement/return is still thin. General esbat summaries and popular moon-water articles are not enough for implementation except as context/restricted mechanics. This lane should wait until source pinning is completed or #171 is worked/folded in.
- **Plant-as-household-companion.** Plant, window, remembrance, and evergreen material are useful, but “plant companion” language must be constrained. The strongest exact Plant support is rosemary remembrance, houseleek as house/roof plant lore, myrtle in window lore, and evergreen winter-house material. Dead-leaf/dormancy forms need either botanical source support or narrower product framing.

### Avoid

- Flame divination, candle-color determinism, oil/herb candle dressing, unsafe flame, unattended flame, smoke cleansing as default, and any fire/smoke rite that creates hazard.
- Ingestible moon water, crystal elixirs, “charged” water for consumption, herbs/crystals in water, or moon-water health/beauty uses.
- Guaranteed cleansing, healing, protection, prosperity, attraction, manifestation, fertility, legal, medical, curse-breaking, or coercive love claims.
- Copying prayers, charms, Wiccan liturgy, Christian/Gaelic sacred language, Indigenous/closed ceremonial forms, or culturally specific ritual texts.
- Turning Home/Plant/Kitchen recommendations into chores, therapy exercises, recipes, or a spell database.

## 2. Priority lane summary

### Candle/light

Readiness: **implementation-ready now** for narrow mechanics; **implementation-ready with restrictions** where using need-fire/new-fire evidence.

Best documented families:

1. **Smoored / banked household fire** — evening lowering, covered ember, safe held continuity, morning rekindling.
2. **Kindling / first light** — a first household flame or light begins the day/moment.
3. **Renewed / new light** — old light is absent or deliberately ended; one new safe light starts the rite.
4. **Light witness / no-flame witness** — candle, lamp, or unlit candle marks attention without divination.

Extraction boundary:

- Use physical mechanics: lower, cover, extinguish, relight, place, return, witness.
- Do not use Gaelic/Christian prayers, Vesta cult theology, disease/purification claims, protection guarantees, or flame behavior interpretation.
- Electric candle/lamp should be first-class, not merely fallback.

### Plant

Readiness: **mixed by subfamily**.

Subfamily readiness:

- **Rosemary / evergreen remembrance:** implementation-ready with restrictions.
- **Houseleek / house-plant-place:** implementation-ready after exact source pinning and safety review.
- **Window/sill plant:** implementation-ready after exact source pinning; myrtle window lore is pinned but luck claims must be stripped.
- **Dead leaf / dormancy:** implementation-ready with restrictions only if framed as botanical observation and safe handling, not as folklore-derived magical outcome.
- **Plant witness:** implementation-ready after exact source pinning.
- **Plant-as-household-companion:** promising but not ready unless stronger exact source support is added.
- **Plant placement/return:** implementation-ready with restrictions when tied to an already safe plant/object and not a care chore.

Extraction boundary:

- Use plant material as place, remembrance, continuity, seasonal state, or household witness.
- Do not use luck/protection/medicine/divination, pregnancy/infant claims, poisonous plant handling, ingestion, or generic “growth metaphor” without material basis.
- Do not default to watering/pruning unless the plant actually needs it.

### Moon-specific

Readiness: **promising but not ready**; at best **implementation-ready after exact source pinning**.

Target families remain correct, but the source basis is not yet hard enough:

1. Moonlit bowl / vessel / jar.
2. Moon-window contact.
3. Moon-timed object placement and return.
4. Full moon holding / acknowledgment.
5. New/dark moon empty vessel or leave-undisturbed forms.
6. Waning return/lowering without forced clearing.

Extraction boundary:

- Moon-specific must stay separate from Candle/light.
- Use “place,” “hold,” “leave,” “cover,” “return,” and “acknowledge,” not “charge,” unless “charge” is later explicitly approved as symbolic-only language.
- No ingestible moon water, crystal elixir, guaranteed cleansing/healing/protection/manifestation/prosperity, or moon phase overriding the check-in contract.

### Home/hearth/table

Readiness: **implementation-ready now** where sourced from hearth/table/object mechanics; **implementation-ready after exact source pinning** for broader household-center material.

Best documented families:

1. Hearth settling / evening closing.
2. Table-center placement.
3. First/last household action.
4. Household object placement and return.
5. House tending without protection guarantee.

Extraction boundary:

- Use hearth/table/center/return as the Home grammar.
- Avoid threshold fallback, cleaning reset, warding/protection guarantees, productivity language, and house-blessing text.

### Reflection word/phrase

Readiness: **mechanics-only** until accessible source sections are pinned.

Best documented families:

1. Written word placed.
2. Written word folded.
3. Written word carried briefly.
4. Written word returned, stored, recycled, or disposed.
5. Plain spoken first/last line.

Extraction boundary:

- Use mechanics only: write, fold, place, carry, return/dispose.
- Do not copy charm/prayer/spell wording, sacred language, medical/protection claims, coercion, or therapy/journaling homework.

### Kitchen shared vessel / table warmth

Readiness: **implementation-ready with restrictions** for shared-vessel mechanics; **promising but not ready** for more specific honey/bread/grain correspondences unless separately pinned.

Best documented families:

1. Shared cup/bowl.
2. Table-center vessel.
3. Bread/grain at table center.
4. Vessel washed/returned as closure.
5. Both-of-us table warmth.

Extraction boundary:

- Use non-alcoholic, ordinary, low-burden materials.
- Avoid alcohol default, recipe burden, food/health advice, prosperity/harvest guarantees, or relationship processing.
