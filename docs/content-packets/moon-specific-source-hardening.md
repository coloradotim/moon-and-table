## 1. Research verdict

PM verdict: **#245 should not green-light broad #236 yet.** The lane is emotionally important and product-important, but the source base is uneven. There are good historical ritual technologies for moon/contact/water symbolism, and there is reviewable modern moon-water practice for jar/bowl/window placement. However, the modern sources are claim-heavy and the stronger historical sources are culturally/religiously specific enough that Moon & Table should treat them as mechanics-only or context, not as directly adopted rites.

What is usable now:

- A narrow **moon-window / moonlit vessel / object-at-window placement** family may proceed only as `implementation-ready with restrictions`.
- The allowed action language should be **place / set / rest / hold / mark / return / acknowledge**, not charge, cleanse, heal, protect, manifest, attract, or empower.
- Any water use should be non-ingestible and symbolic; Moon & Table should prefer empty bowl, covered vessel, or dry object placement over water when possible.

What is not ready:

- Broad moon-water rituals are **not ready** as production content.
- Crystal elixir / crystal-in-water practice is **avoid**.
- General esbat and Drawing Down the Moon material is not a safe active source for household two-person Moon-specific material practice.
- Dark/new moon “leave undisturbed” and waning “return/lowering” are promising, but need better source pinning before active implementation.

Recommendation: **Do not open broad #236 implementation yet.** Either narrow #236 to source-pinned symbolic placement/return only, or run one additional focused source pass for modern, reviewable lunar-practice books with exact page locations.

## 5. Approved / restricted / avoid classifications

### Approved with restrictions

- **Moon-window placement:** acceptable if framed as setting/placing/resting a vessel or object where moonlight can meet it.
- **Moonlit vessel:** acceptable if dry, empty, covered, or non-ingestible.
- **Return action:** acceptable as a neutral closure step.

### Restricted / mechanics-only

- Chinese moon-mirror/fangzhu material: extract only the mechanics of moon-facing receptacle/contact/return. Do not import Chinese ritual office, sacrifice, cosmology, Daoist alchemy, medicine, or “bright water” claims.
- Modern moon-water articles: extract only jar/bowl/window/porch placement. Do not extract claims, ingredients, crystals, beauty uses, ingestion, healing, cleansing, prosperity, or manifesting.
- Wiccan esbat / Drawing Down / Cakes and Wine: context only unless future source review approves a non-initiatory modern source. Do not copy rites.

### Avoid

- Ingestible moon water.
- Crystal elixirs or crystals placed in water.
- “Charge” language unless explicitly retained as a quoted/source term in research notes only.
- Cleansing/healing/protection/manifestation/prosperity/attraction claims.
- Hekate Deipnon offerings, dead/crossroads/spirit appeasement, or household purification claims.
- Copied prayers, invocations, sacred language, deity embodiment, or gendered/sexualized Wiccan ritual mechanics.
- Outdoor vessel placement that could be unsafe, contaminated, knocked over, frozen, overheated, or accessible to animals/children.

## 8. Safety / cultural / copyright cautions

Safety:

- Do not recommend drinking moon water.
- Do not recommend crystal elixirs.
- Do not place crystals in water.
- Do not recommend adding herbs, salt, flowers, oils, alcohol, or smoke unless separately reviewed.
- Avoid outdoor placement unless the app can keep the instruction safe: stable surface, covered vessel, no ingestion, retrieve promptly, away from pets/children.
- Avoid sprays, diffusers, skin use, bath use, or beauty use.
- Avoid medical, therapeutic, veterinary, allergy, pregnancy, or health claims.

Cultural/religious:

- Chinese moon-mirror / fangzhu material is culturally and historically specific. Use only as source context for moon-facing receptacle mechanics, not as a borrowed rite.
- Gardnerian/Wiccan material is religious and in some cases initiatory or orphaned; do not copy structure, prayers, deity invocation, gendered/sexualized mechanics, or circle rites.
- Hekate Deipnon / Noumenia context is culturally specific. Do not adapt Hekate offerings, offerings to the dead, purification/atonement, crossroads, or “do not look back” mechanics.

Copyright:

- InStyle, Allure, modern translations, and modern Wiccan publications are copyrighted. Use short internal summaries only; do not copy wording.
- Sacred Texts Gardnerian BoS page states the text’s copyright status is orphaned. Treat as context only and avoid active reproduction.
